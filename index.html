David Freeman

Writer in Minneapolis, Minnesota.

About

My work has appeared in The Kenyon Review, Massachusetts Review, and Prairie Schooner. I am a graduate of the Helen Zell Writers’ Program at the University of Michigan and a recipient of the Theodore Roethke Award, the Gianna Luschei Award, and the Biggett-Swain Prize.

Current Projects

Actress Must Have No Mouth
A novel-in-stories about Magnum photographers documenting the filming of The Misfits.

The Gospels of James Jesus Angleton
A collection of poetry centered on the life and legacy of James Jesus Angleton, first counterintelligence director for the CIA.

Full manuscripts are available upon request.
